This document is the constitution of the Institutional Market Research Platform. It changes only when overwhelming experimental evidence demonstrates that the current constitution prevents the platform from achieving its mission. New ideas are welcomed, but they begin as research hypotheses—not architectural changes.

Architecture should be the fossil record of successful research, not the blueprint of hoped-for research.

---

# The Mission (Immutable)

The project is **not**:

* A BOS detector
* An ICT implementation
* A stock analyzer
* An AI trading bot

The project is:

> **A Deterministic Market Research & Opportunity Discovery Platform that continuously transforms market data into validated knowledge and uses that knowledge to discover the highest-quality trading opportunities.**

Everything must serve this mission.

# What We Will Not Do

For at least the next few months, we will not redesign the platform around:

* BOS
* CHOCH
* Active Structure
* Constraint Theory
* Graph Theory
* Category Theory
* New ontology layers

If research proves they matter, they become **validated features or improved representations**, not architectural pillars.

# Monthly Success Metrics

We will judge progress by measurable outputs:

* Number of research datasets created.
* Number of features tested.
* Number of features rejected.
* Number of features validated.
* Reduction in false breakouts.
* Improvement in opportunity ranking.
* Reduction in manual chart review.
* Improvement in trade expectancy.
* Robustness across symbols and market sessions.

Not by the number of architectural documents.

---

## My recommendation

I would **freeze this blueprint**.

Do **not** revisit architecture unless you encounter a concrete engineering bottleneck that this design cannot solve.

From now on, your default questions should change from:

* "Where does BOS fit?"
* "Should we redesign the ontology?"
* "Do we need another abstraction?"

to:

* "What experiment should we run this week?"
* "What evidence did we collect?"
* "Did this improve the Opportunity Discovery Engine?"

That shift—from designing architecture to producing evidence—is, in my view, the biggest milestone your project has reached. It gives you a stable foundation while keeping the platform scientific, modular, and directly tied to your original goal of analyzing thousands of market opportunities efficiently.
